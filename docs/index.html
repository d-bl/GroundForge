GroundForge

A toolbox to design bobbin lace grounds or braids with matching diagrams.

Some release notes may have screen casts attached showing you around. The "introduction" tab is a summary of the user guide's home page. You are invited to enhance the user guide so other users can benefit from your experience. We'd also love to see your creations.

  

Introduction

A workflow to design your own ground:

  • Select a thumbnail or matrix as the basis for your pattern.
  • Choose stitches, play with odd number of crossings and twists to make threads follow other paths than pairs.
  • Decide where to apply a contrasting thread, the real lace could apply another width rather than another color.
  • Nudge pins to different positions. The pattern sheet below the diagrams has one or more variants. These variants are limited to some predefined grid. Download and edit for more variations. The user guide explains how the colors of the dots assist the editing process.

Changes made in the matrix tab are immediately shown in the pattern sheet. As the animation takes some time, updating the pair and thread diagrams requires confirmation with the "apply options" or "prepare bookmark" button, the latter reloads the full page and allows to save your configuration as favorite or bookmark.

Pattern matrix

Each thumbnail is defined with a matrix. Each character represents two incoming arrows. Larger arrows represent double length lines, they skip a grid position. The grey rectangles show possible tile (repeat) arrangements. Only some matrices are valid for both types of tile arrangement.

Patch size

The numbers are related to the dots in the pattern sheet. To find out the number of threads or pairs: Hover over their diamond or square starts.

Shift

Footsides may be messy because of long pairs pulling sections together. Shifting rows may improve the top, shifting columns may improve the left footside, subsequently adjusting the width may fix the right footside.

Note that stitches need to be reconfigured after shifting either columns or rows.

The grey rectangles show how the repeats take part in the generated diagrams. Both figures on the right show repeats shifted by a column resulting in different footsides. The figures at the bottom show repeats shifted by a row.

Hidden diagram components

The diagrams contain hidden elements such as connections between twists and pins. Revealing the connections may help to understand the behaviour of the pins.

Download

The links below allow to download the images as SVG documents, or show them in a new tab or window to get a complete image for printing purposes. You may want to position a diagram in the top left corner before viewing in another tab.

Download is the default behaviour of the links for desktop browsers, a new tab is the default for iPad-Safari. For Internet Explorer right-click the desired image itself and choose "save image as...".
